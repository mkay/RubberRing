Rubber Ring is a tool for practicing song parts. Pick an audio file, mark a start/stop region on its waveform, and play it back in a seamless, gapless loop. It auto-detects tempo and beats to snap the loop markers to a beat grid, with full manual override.

Early-stage release — expect rough edges. Beat detection and count-in still need improvement. Feedback and bug reports welcome via Issues.

Features:

* Import-and-own library — picked files are copied into app-private storage, so loops never break when the original is moved, renamed, or deleted.
* Waveform region selection with gapless looping — drag start/stop handles to mark a section; the loop boundary wraps with zero audible gap or click.
* Automatic, hand-editable beat grid — a dependency-free estimator finds BPM and a downbeat, and handles snap to the beat grid.
* Adjustable playback speed — slow a tricky passage down (or speed it up) without changing pitch.
* Count-in — long-press Play to start after a metronome counts you in at the track's tempo.
* Saved loops and practice arrangements — capture named sections per track and chain them into a sequence, each repeated as many times as you set.
* Backup & restore — export your whole library (tracks and saved loops) to a file and restore it after a reinstall or on another device.
* Light & dark themes, follow-playhead scrolling, and a recents list.

No ads, no trackers, no network access.
